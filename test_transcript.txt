Here and Now - Interview with State Senator Duey Stroebel

HOST: Welcome to Here and Now. I'm Zac Schultz. Today we're joined by State Senator Duey Stroebel, Republican from Cedarburg, to discuss workforce development challenges facing Wisconsin manufacturers.

Senator, thanks for joining us.

SENATOR STROEBEL: Thanks for having me, Zac.

HOST: Wisconsin manufacturers have been vocal about workforce shortages. What are you hearing from businesses in your district?

SENATOR STROEBEL: Well, the number one issue I hear about is finding qualified workers. We have jobs available, good-paying jobs with benefits, but we can't find people with the right skills. Many manufacturers are turning away business because they simply don't have enough workers.

HOST: You've proposed legislation to expand apprenticeship programs. Can you explain that?

SENATOR STROEBEL: Absolutely. We need to create more pathways for young people to enter manufacturing careers. Our bill would provide tax credits to companies that create new apprenticeship positions and would also fund partnerships between technical colleges and manufacturers to develop industry-specific training programs.

HOST: Critics say we should be investing more in traditional four-year degrees. How do you respond?

SENATOR STROEBEL: I have nothing against four-year degrees - they're valuable for many career paths. But we also need to recognize that skilled trades offer excellent career opportunities. A trained machinist or welder can earn $60,000 to $80,000 a year, often without student loan debt. We need to stop stigmatizing vocational education and recognize it as a legitimate and valuable path.

HOST: What about companies? Are they willing to invest in training?

SENATOR STROEBEL: Many are, but it's expensive and time-consuming. That's why the tax credits are important - they help offset the cost of training. We're also looking at ways to streamline regulations that make it difficult for companies to take on apprentices.

HOST: Any timeline on when this legislation might move forward?

SENATOR STROEBEL: We're working with leadership now. I'm hoping we can include it in the budget or move it as a standalone bill this session. This is too important to wait.

HOST: Senator Duey Stroebel, thanks for your time.

SENATOR STROEBEL: Thank you, Zac.
